History â€‹

In the midst of the Cold War, when the USA and the USSR were competing for space, humanity made a revolutionary decision: space must belong to everyone.

The Treaty on Principles Governing the Activities of States in the Exploration and Use of Outer Space, including the Moon and Other Celestial Bodies (full name) was ratified in 1967 and became the foundation of space law.

Key Principles: â€‹

  1. Space â€” the common heritage of mankind

    • No state can appropriate the Moon, planets, or outer space.
    • It is the property of all mankind.

  2. Peaceful Use

    • Prohibition of placing weapons of mass destruction in space.
    • Space objects must be used exclusively for peaceful purposes.

  3. Freedom of Research

    • Every country has the right to explore space.
    • There is no monopoly on knowledge about space.

  4. Responsibility for Activities

    • States bear responsibility for their space activities.
    • Including activities of private companies.

  5. International Cooperation

    • States are obligated to inform each other about their activities.
    • Astronauts are considered "envoys of mankind".
